Hi, I’m Darcel. I learned first hand what it takes to get out of homelessness.

When I was injured and lost my job as a welder working on the new Bay Bridge, I became homeless. I realized the longer I stayed in that condition, the harder it would become to escape it. I managed to find and take cooking classes, start a catering company, and escaped poverty.

Living on the streets, you spend most of your time trying to survive. It’s hard to find the resources and time to get trained in a new field and search for jobs. Now I want to help people who are where I was find the resources they need to get their life back.

Since 2015, we've equipped one shelter and 7 SROs with free WiFi, providing more than 1000 underserved people with access to the Internet. And that's just the beginning.

The Lifeline Program offers free smartphones to low-income people, but without access to the internet the impact is limited. We partner with local internet service providers to offer free WiFi with the goal of connecting every shelter in the US so that people can access the resources, training, and jobs that will help them get their lives back.

In 2016, we’re building AskDarcel, an app that leverages access to smartphones and WiFI, to provide those in need with a virtual case manager, guiding users through a step-by-step process to improve their lives.

After losing my home, I realised how important it was to create a daily routine and focus my efforts on finding a job. AskDarcel will help low-income and no-income people connect with the resources they need on a daily basis: food, shelter, personal hygiene, healthcare and more.

ShelterTech and Team

ShelterTech is a non-profit organization in which all board members are volunteers. Income from events and fundraising are invested into improving and maintaining the technology and in employing members of the homeless community to support our efforts.

ShelterTech works to overcome the digital divide for low-income and no-income individuals by distributing free WiFi in shelters and creating AskDarcel. We count on a multidisciplinary team of professionals who share their expertise and networks to help fight homelessness.
